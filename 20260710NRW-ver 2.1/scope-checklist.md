# Scope Assessment: 6-Factor Checklist

Adapted from Dhillon (2022) Fig.1, FEBS Journal.

## Assessment

Rate each factor 1 (poor) to 5 (excellent). Any score 2 requires discussion before proceeding.

### 1. Personal Motivation
**Is the author team interested and enthusiastic about this topic?**

- 5: Deeply passionate -- this is your core research area
- 3: Moderately interested -- related to your work
- 1: Writing this only because you were asked

### 2. Timeliness
**Has there been a sufficient body of recent research?**

- 5: 20+ relevant papers published in the last 5 years; active research field
- 3: 10-15 papers; field is moderately active
- 1: Fewer than 10 recent papers; field may be stagnant or too niche

### 3. Novelty
**Can the author offer a new perspective on this topic?**

- 5: No similar review in past 2 years; you have a genuinely new angle
- 3: Similar review exists, but you can cover from a different perspective or include substantial new material
- 1: Multiple recent reviews cover the same ground; nothing new to add

### 4. Breadth
**Is the topic focused enough to be manageable but broad enough to capture readership?**

- 5: Well-defined scope; can be covered deeply in 4000-6000 words
- 3: Slightly too broad or too narrow; may need scope adjustment
- 1: Either impossibly broad (textbook territory) or too narrow (few readers)

### 5. Journal Relevance
**Is the topic relevant to the target journal audience?**

- 5: Directly matches journal scope and reader interests
- 3: Partially relevant; some readers would be interested
- 1: Mismatch between topic and journal audience

### 6. Author Expertise
**Is the author team adequately knowledgeable about this topic?**

- 5: You have published primary research in this specific area
- 3: You work in a related field and understand the core concepts
- 1: This is a new area for you; consider adding an experienced co-author
