# Narrative Review vs Systematic Review

## Comparison Table

| Feature | Narrative Review | Systematic Review |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Purpose** | Broad overview; track development of scientific principles/clinical concepts | Answer a specific, focused clinical/research question |
| **Scope** | Wider -- allows exploration of broader topics | Narrow -- tightly focused PICO question |
| **Search** | Not standardized; author-driven; may not be disclosed | Exhaustive, reproducible, multi-database; fully documented |
| **Study Selection** | Author judgment; may introduce selection bias | Pre-defined inclusion/exclusion criteria; independent dual screening |
| **Quality Assessment** | Usually informal or absent | Formal risk-of-bias assessment (e.g., Cochrane RoB 2, NOS) |
| **Synthesis** | Qualitative narrative; identifies themes, gaps, new insights | Qualitative or quantitative (meta-analysis); structured methods |
| **Bias Risk** | Higher -- author may selectively cite supporting evidence | Lower -- explicit methodology reduces bias |
| **Reproducibility** | Low -- another author would produce a different review | High -- similar protocol leads to similar conclusions |
| **Guidelines** | No formal reporting standard | PRISMA 2020, Cochrane Handbook, JBI Manual |
| **Registration** | Usually not registered | PROSPERO or similar registry recommended |
| **Time Required** | Months (literature immersion + writing) | 6-18 months |
| **Best For** | Developing conceptual frameworks; tracing historical evolution; identifying gaps across a broad field | Informing clinical guidelines; answering specific intervention questions |

## When Narrative Review Is the Right Choice

- Topic is too broad for a focused systematic review question
- Trace the development of a scientific concept over time
- Synthesize mechanistic evidence across multiple levels (molecular to cellular to animal to human)
- Develop a new conceptual framework or model
- Identify research gaps across an entire field

## When Systematic Review Is Better

- Specific clinical question (PICO-formulated)
- Need to inform clinical practice guidelines
- Want to quantify an effect size via meta-analysis
- Regulatory or policy decision requiring highest evidence level

## This Skill's Position

This skill writes **narrative reviews only**. For systematic reviews, use PRISMA-based workflows. Some systematic review methodology (structured search, reproducible strategy) has been adapted into this skill's narrative review workflow where it adds rigor without constraining scope.
